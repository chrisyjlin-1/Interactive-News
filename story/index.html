Growth of interest in American women's college volleyball

Over the past decade, the interest in women's collegiate volleyball has grown significantly, especially in Division I NCAA volleyball. Leading the charge is the University of Nebraska, who have led the average home attendance each year since 2013. Northwestern University also has its own B10 Women's volleyball team.

Average home attendance

The University of Hawaii and the University of Nebraska's teams have led the average home attendance rates since 1998. The University of Hawaii led the average attendance record until 2013 when the University of Nebraska overtook it. Both teams have historically done well in the NCAA Championships and have won the title more than once.

One of the biggest moments in women's college volleyball was when Nebraska hosted its women's volleyball team at Memorial Stadium. It sold out, and they set a new record for attendance: 92,003, according to the NCAA.

Northwestern has its own DI women's volleyball team. During its last season in 2025, the team finished with a 7-13 record in Big Ten games and a winning 17-15 overall record, according to Northwestern Athletics. The team also sold out Welsh-Ryan Arena for the first time in 2024 when they went up against Nebraska.
